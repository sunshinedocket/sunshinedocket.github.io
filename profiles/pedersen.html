Sen. Jamie Pedersen

D-43 | Senate Majority Leader | EVP & General Counsel, McKinstry Co.
Possible Conflict
The core finding: Pedersen is the Executive Vice President and General Counsel of McKinstry Co., a construction and energy services company whose entities received $29.1 million in state agency payments in a single year. He chairs the Senate Ways & Means Committee, which controls the operating budgets of the agencies paying his employer. He also sits on the Legislative Ethics Board that would investigate any conflict complaint against him.

The Numbers

From Pedersen's own signed F1 disclosure (submission 132680, filed March 24, 2026), McKinstry entities disclosed $29,119,211 in payments from Washington state agencies during calendar year 2025. This is not revenue from private clients or the open market. This is public money flowing from agencies whose budgets Pedersen directly controls through Ways & Means.

While receiving this volume of state payments, Pedersen's employer promoted him from General Counsel to Executive Vice President. His reported income jumped from $200K-$500K (CY2024) to $500K-$750K (CY2025). He was given more money and a bigger title at the same time his committee was funding the agencies that write checks to McKinstry.

The Structural Problem

The Ways & Means Committee sets the operating budget for every state agency. When Ways & Means allocates more money to the University of Washington, DES, or WSDOT, those agencies have more money to spend on construction, energy services, and building leases. McKinstry provides construction, energy services, and building leases to those agencies.

Pedersen does not need to steer a specific contract to McKinstry. He does not need to call an agency head. He does not need to do anything improper at all. The structural incentive is built into the org chart: the man who sets agency budgets earns $500K-$750K from a company that depends on those budgets for $29 million in annual revenue.

Meanwhile, DES awarded McKinstry Master Contract #04224 for HVAC Services, effective September 10, 2025. Master contracts are state-negotiated agreements that allow agencies to purchase services without individual procurement. Once McKinstry holds a master contract, every agency in the state can buy from them with minimal oversight.

The Ethics Board

Pedersen sits on the Legislative Ethics Board. If a citizen filed a conflict-of-interest complaint about a legislator, the Ethics Board would evaluate it. If someone filed that complaint about Pedersen, he would be a member of the body reviewing it.

Washington does not require Ethics Board members to recuse themselves from complaints involving their own conduct. The board operates on a self-policing model.
