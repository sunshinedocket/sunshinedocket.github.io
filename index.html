What we found

Every member of the Washington State Legislature files a financial disclosure. It lists who pays them, who pays their spouse, and whether anyone in their household is a registered lobbyist. The state also publishes every campaign donation and every lobbyist registration. All public. All on data.wa.gov.

Nobody connects them.

We built a tool that does. It cross-references all three databases for every sitting legislator and flags cases where the people writing the laws have financial ties to the people and industries affected by those laws.

A Senate leader earns $500K-$750K from a company that received $29 million from state agencies whose budgets he controls. A committee chair's consulting firm was paid $87,000 by the union that lobbies her committee, and the two government filings describe the same payments with two different labels. A House leader's spouse is a registered lobbyist for his largest campaign donor. A minority leader manages a personal investment fund whose holdings are invisible to voters while he sets tax policy for the industries his fund invests in.

These are not accusations. They are public records, exposed by cross-referencing databases the state keeps separate. Every number links to its source. Every claim is independently verifiable. If we got something wrong, the citation trail makes it findable.

We publish both parties. We publish clean passes alongside findings. The methodology is the same for everyone.
